# When You Want to Quit

*Written 2026-07-04, the day this all still felt exciting — addressed to the version of me reading it in week 10 or 11.*

You are probably here around Phase 4–5: mid-semester, the novelty is gone, Spring Security is being Spring Security, and a newer, shinier project idea is whispering. **This moment was predicted on day 0. Nothing is wrong. This is the scheduled boss fight, not a sign the project was a mistake.**

## Facts, not pep

**1. There is no "half-finished" state.** The phases were shaped so every exit is a stable asset:

| If you stop after… | You still walk away with |
|---|---|
| Phase 3 (Checkpoint A) | a complete, deployed payments system with a double-entry ledger — already ahead of nearly every fresh-grad portfolio |
| Phase 4 | …plus a real product UI and auth |
| Phase 5 | …plus a KYC pipeline — the compliance story has begun |
| Phase 6 | the full flagship: the sentence in `docs/blueprint.md` becomes entirely true |

You never promised 18 weeks. You only ever promise **the current phase** — two to three weeks, once.

**2. Your failure mode was designed against.** You quit projects at decision-fatigue points and when new ideas compete. Here, decisions were made in July (ADRs), work is pre-cut into cards (open card → follow → tick — the same mechanic that makes games finishable), and new ideas have a home that is *inside* the project (`docs/scope.md` Icebox). The shiny idea is not the enemy of LedgerPay; it is an Icebox entry.

**3. The deadlines are not decorative.** October 2026: KL internship applications — Checkpoint A is the ticket. April 2027: graduation, then Hong Kong. The cost of quitting is not the sunk hours; it is walking into HK fintech interviews indistinguishable from everyone else who "knows Spring Boot".

## The rules for this exact moment

1. **Shrink, don't quit.** If a card has stalled for two sessions, cut the card in half and finish the smaller half today.
2. **Momentum over mood.** Two weeks of zero progress → schedule one 45-minute session and tick the smallest checkbox on the current card. Do not wait to feel like it.
3. **Shiny idea?** Write it in the Icebox with today's date. It is parked, not killed.
4. Reread `docs/blueprint.md` → "The five-minute demo scene". That scene is real, it is close, and it is yours.

*— you, with full context and full enthusiasm*
